# You and Your Research

Author: Richard W. Hamming
Source: https://www.cs.virginia.edu/~robins/YouAndYourResearch.html
Original venue: Bell Communications Research Colloquium Seminar
Date: March 7, 1986
Local note: Source note with relevant excerpt and context, not a full republication.

## Relevant Context

Hamming's talk discusses how researchers choose important problems, develop taste, tolerate ambiguity, and compound knowledge over a career. The lunch-table story in `how_to_be_good_at_research.md` refers to Hamming asking colleagues what the important problems in their fields were, what important problems they were working on, and why they were working on problems they did not expect to matter.

The same talk is also the source for the compound-interest framing of knowledge and productivity, and it quotes Pasteur's line that luck favors the prepared mind.
